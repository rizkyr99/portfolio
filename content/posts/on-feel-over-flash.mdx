---
title: "On feel over flash"
date: "2026-03-02"
summary: "Why the guitars I come back to are the plain-looking ones — and what that has to do with software."
---

The Telecaster is a plank with two pickups. It has one tone knob, one volume knob,
a three-way switch, and a bridge that's basically a metal tray. It's been made more
or less the same way since 1951.

People have tried to improve it for seventy-five years. Most of the "improvements"
don't stick. That's not because players are nostalgic — it's because the tool was
right the first time. The limitations turned out to be features. You can't hide
behind it, so you get better.

I think good software has the same shape. Tools that do one thing with a clear
voice, that you can learn the whole of, that hold up under twenty-year use. Less
decoration. More touch.
